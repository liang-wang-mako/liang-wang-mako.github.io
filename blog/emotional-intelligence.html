Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is the ability to understand, use, and manage your own emotions in positive ways to relieve stress, communicate effectively, empathize with others, overcome challenges and defuse conflict. EQ helps you build stronger relationships, succeed at school and work, and achieve your career and personal goals. It can also help you to connect with your feelings, turn intention into action, and make informed decisions about what matters most to you.

Emotional intelligence is an important life skill

Emotional intelligence helps us navigate emotions not just in ourselves, but also in others. A recent paper published in the Journal of Leadership and Organisational Studies has revealed that individuals with generally higher levels of emotional intelligence experienced lower levels of stress while leading remotely during the pandemic.

Having a good understanding of emotional intelligence helps us make better decisions and use our emotions to work smarter. Additionally, it helps us become better communicators, interact better with people, and facilitates a greater understanding of ourselves and the emotions of others. Finally, it have significant benefits to our ability to learn, perform, and get hired.

How do we Cultivate Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is not something you are born with. It is something that we learn. What we think, do, and pay attention to changes the structure and function of our brains. In the same way that we train the muscles in our body, we can train and change our brain. As with any skill, we can train our Emotional Intelligence skills.

We can cultivate emotional intelligence by practicing mindfulness, taking time to reflect on our emotions, being self-aware, developing empathy, recognizing and managing triggers, practicing active listening, and engaging in open and honest conversations. We can also cultivate emotional intelligence through self-improvement activities such as journaling, reading books, taking classes and attending seminars, and engaging in activities that foster self-care. Being aware of our moments of emotional intensity and developing self-management skills can also help us cultivate emotional intelligence. /p>

How to use emotional intelligence every day?

Using emotional intelligence in everyday life involves becoming self-aware, practicing active listening, cultivating empathy, and managing our emotions to respond wisely to people and situations.

    Here are some tips for using emotional intelligence every day:
  • Take time to reflect and practice mindfulness
  • Listen to others attentively and practice active listening
  • Become aware of your feelings and those of those around you
  • Strive to understand and empathize with other people's perspectives
  • Practice self-regulation by recognizing your own triggers and responding calmly
  • Identify patterns that lead to challenging situations and work to prevent them
  • Communicate your feelings in an appropriate way
  • Practice self-care activities such as journaling or taking a break when needed
