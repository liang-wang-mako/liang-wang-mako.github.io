Reflection

  • What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

    • The three new things I have learnt about myself and my ego due to the core learning are:

    Awareness of self-limiting beliefs: At the beginning of the course, I found I needed to write two or three blogs, and provide reflections on lot of questions every week. I was really worry about that as I thought I was not good at English writing and I didn't like to write in English. Through emotional intelligence exploration, I became aware of the emotions associated with self-limiting beliefs. Negative emotions like fear, self-doubt, or dislike often accompany these beliefs. I suddenly understood the reasons I was not good at writing in English were my belief itself and the emotion that I didn't like to write in English.

    Recognition of the potential for change: Through Learning about neuroplasticity, I understood my brain had remarkable capacity to adapt and change. This understanding has led me to realize that my ego and its associated beliefs are not fixed or unchangeable. I have discovered that I have the ability to rewire my thinking patterns, challenge limiting beliefs, and develop new perspectives. This recognition opens up a world of possibilities for personal growth and transformation. My improvement in English writing is just an example.

    Shifting perspective on failure and challenges: A growth mindset means viewing setbacks, failures, and challenges as opportunities for learning and growth. Through the core learning process, I have begun to developed a new perspective on these experiences. Rather than seeing them as personal flaws or reasons for self-criticism, I have learned to approach them with curiosity, resilience, and a desire to learn. This shift in perspective can lead to increased self-compassion and a willingness to step outside my comfort zone, ultimately fostering personal growth and development. I am feeling I like to write in English.

  • What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

    • Values: Values are the guiding principles and beliefs that shape our behavior and decision-making. In learning, values help me prioritize what knowledge and skills are important to acquire, and remind me team's sucess is more important the individual success. In programming, values guide ethical decision-making, such as respecting user privacy, ensuring data security, and developing inclusive and accessible software.

    Empathy: Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings and perspectives of others. In learning, empathy helps me understand others' challenges and needs, engage in collaborative learning, and build supportive relationships. In programming, empathy helps me design and develop software that meets the needs of users effectively.

    Self-awareness: Self-awareness is the ability to introspect and understand one's own thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. In learning, self-awareness helps me understand my learning style, strengths, weaknesses, and preferences. In programming, self-awareness helps me to identify my biases, assumptions, and limitations.

  • Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

    • I think following reasons can explain why our school are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course:

    Firstly, core skills is about growing the human part of the web development equation.

    Secondly, understanding ourself in the context of others and utilising our Human Skills will set us up for our career. Tech employers aren't looking for top grades or the person with the most outstanding code. They're looking for exceptional human beings who will enhance the culture of their team.

    Additionally, hiring managers agree that training a person to do the job is relatively easy, but teaching them how to communicate, cooperate, and work amongst diverse perspectives is much more complex. That's where Core and Human Skills come in.

    Finally, core will introduce us to techniques to train our mind. We'll learn valuable ways of understanding problems and support to strengthen our self-solving skills. Core also prepares us for learning experiences drawing from our agency of values and strengths. It is the culmination of values, awareness, and mindset to maximise our learning and career journey.

  • Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

    • So far, in our core learning process, we have learned:

  • identity, values, and strengths,
  • learning plan,
  • emotional intelligence,
  • neuroplasticity
  • a growth mindset

    • Studying core learning can be incredibly valuable and complementary to practicing programming. Here are a few justifications for dedicating time to studying core learning:

    Self-awareness and personal growth: Core learning helps us explore and understand our identity, values, and strengths. This self-reflection is crucial in guiding our career choices, setting meaningful goals, and aligning our actions with your values. By gaining clarity on our personal attributes and motivations, we can make informed decisions about the programming path that best suits our interests and goals.

    Learning efficiency and effectiveness: Making a learning plan helps us structure our learning journey, set achievable goals, identify the resources and steps needed to progress, optimize our learning process, track our progress, and stay focused on acquiring the relevant programming skills aligned with our goals.

    Emotional intelligence and interpersonal skills: Cultivating emotional intelligence enhances our ability to understand and manage emotions, both in ourselves and in others. This skill is valuable in any professional setting, including programming. Emotional intelligence enables effective communication, collaboration, and problem-solving. By cultivating emotional intelligence, we can build strong relationships, foster a positive work environment, and enhance our overall professional effectiveness as a programmer.

    Mental resilience and growth mindset: Understanding neuroplasticity and cultivating a growth mindset are powerful tools for personal and professional development. They allow us to embrace challenges, persist in the face of setbacks, and continually learn and improve our programming skills. By adopting a growth mindset, we view obstacles as opportunities for growth, approach learning with enthusiasm, and maintain resilience in the face of programming challenges.

    In conclusion, by dedicating time to core learning alongside programming practice, we can develop a well-rounded skill set that goes beyond technical proficiency. These aspects contribute to our personal and professional growth, enhance our problem-solving abilities, and foster effective collaboration and communication. Ultimately, this comprehensive approach can lead to long-term success and satisfaction in our programming career.
