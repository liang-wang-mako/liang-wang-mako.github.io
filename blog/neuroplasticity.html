Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

In this blog, I would like to discuss my understanding of Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset and how it influences your learning.

  • What Is Neuroplasticity?

    • Neuroplasticity is also known as neural plasticity or brain plasticity, refers to the brain's ability to change and reorganize its structure and function in response to experiences, learning, and environmental factors.

    Until recently, the conventional thinking was that our brains were hard-wired at birth and therefore unchangeable. In actuality, our brains are like malleable plastic that is constantly being reshaped by our daily experiences.

    Science has also revealed that our beliefs about ourselves, what we think and feel about our abilities and our beliefs about 'success' and 'failure' hugely influence our outcomes.

  • How does neuroplasticity work?

    • The human brain is composed of individual working units or vast networks called neurons. Millions of interconnected neurons work together to accomplish a single task.

    The neural networks follow specific and unique connectivity patterns, firing in equally specific sequences, and neurons help individuals complete various tasks.

    Frequently used neurons have stronger connections in the brain, while never or rarely-used neurons eventually die. When weak connections prune away, and new connections are made, the brain becomes adaptable to changing circumstances and environments.

    According to neurologist and educator Judy Willis (and suggested by a research-rich chapter in the second edition of Developmental Psychopathology, among many other publications), neuroplasticity is defined as the selective organising of connections between neurons in our brains.

    When people repeatedly practice an activity or access a memory, their neural networks -- groups of neurons that fire together, creating electrochemical pathways -- shape themselves according to that activity or memory. When people stop practising new things, the brain will eventually eliminate, or "prune," the connecting cells that formed the pathways. Like in a system of freeways connecting various cities, the more cars going to a particular destination, the wider the road that carries them needs to be. However, the fewer cars travelling that way, the fewer lanes are required.

      There are two main types of neuroplasticity:
    • Functional plasticity is the ability of the brain to move functions to an entire brain area from a damaged area of the brain. It is initiated by learning and development, resulting in permanent structural changes in neural synapses.
    • Structural plasticity is a change in the strength of neuron connections in the brain. The brain can alter its physical structure in response to learning and the brain's ability to change its neuronal connections.

  • Benefits of Understanding the Principles of Neuroplasticity

    • Remembering the brain's capacity to change can help you realize you can respond to stress, anxiety, frustration, or disappointment differently than you did before. This, in turn, can help you break thought and behavioral patterns.

    While much of life may feel out of control, there are many other things you can control. We can change. What you do, fear, or think doesn't have to be permanent.

    If your thoughts or beliefs are causing you anxiety, for example, you have the innate ability to change those thoughts and beliefs.You can change unwanted habits, too, and develop new ones.

  • Growth Mindset

    • A mindset is the series of beliefs people hold about themselves; their self-perception. A growth mindset is the belief that one's abilities, intelligence, and talents can be developed and improved through dedication, effort, and a willingness to learn. Individuals with a growth mindset understand that their basic qualities are not fixed traits but can be cultivated and developed over time.

  • Fixed Mindset

    • In contrast, a fixed mindset is the belief that one's abilities and intelligence are fixed traits and cannot be significantly changed. Individuals with a fixed mindset tend to believe that their talents and abilities are innate, and they often avoid challenges, fear failure, and feel discouraged when faced with setbacks or criticism.

  • Why Is a Growth Mindset Important?

    • Having a “growth mindset” has never been more important than now. The world is changing at an incredibly fast pace, and there are new challenges we must face every day. Our way of life is changing, norms are changing, and we must keep up and adjust to all of these changes. This is why having a growth mindset is so necessary.

    It is easy to see that a growth mindset is very beneficial:

    Individuals with a growth mindset see challenges as opportunities for growth, and they embrace them rather than shy away from them. They understand that effort and persistence are essential for improvement and success. This mindset helps them stay motivated, overcome obstacles, and bounce back from failures.

    Embracing a growth mindset is helpful to effective learning and skill development. When individuals believe that their abilities can be developed, they are more likely to engage in deliberate practice, seek out challenges, and apply themselves to learn new skills. They approach learning with a positive attitude, view mistakes as learning opportunities, and are more willing to put in the necessary effort to improve.

    In a word, a growth mindset promotes a positive attitude towards learning and development, enhances motivation and resilience, and enables individuals to adapt and thrive in a changing world. By adopting a growth mindset, individuals can unlock their full potential, cultivate their abilities, and achieve greater success and fulfillment in various aspects of life.

  • How will I integrate a growth mindset into My learning journey?

    • I integrate a growth mindset into My learning journey by:

    Embracing tasks that are slightly outside my comfort zone and require effort and perseverance to overcome like . Embracing challenges helps me develop new skills and stretch your abilities.

    Change my perspective on failure. See it as an opportunity for learning and growth rather than a reflection of your abilities. Analyze your mistakes, extract lessons from them, and apply those lessons to improve your future performance.

  • Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset Are Relevant

    • Neuroplasticity and growth mindset are related concepts that both involve the brain's ability to adapt and change over time.

    “A growth mindset” refers to the idea that you can always change and grow. The word “neuroplasticity” refers to your brain's ability to change and grow over the time when exposed to learning new things. You can connect the concepts by saying that a growth mindset encourages you to try new things to help your brain function better.

    In a word, engaging in practices related to a growth mindset can benefit your brain's neuroplasticity; uour brain's ability to adapt to change enhances your growth mindset belief.
